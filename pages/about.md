<p data-i18n="en">Hi, I'm Ruijie. You can also call me Jerry. I am glad you made it here. It means my somewhat "unconventional" background caught your attention enough to dig deeper, and you decided to give it a shot. I really appreciate that, especially in this AI era.</p>
<p data-i18n="zh">你好，我是 Ruijie，你也可以叫我 Jerry。很高兴你能看到这里——这说明我这段略显"非典型"的背景成功引起了你的兴趣，让你愿意继续深入了解，并决定给我一个机会。尤其是在如今这个 AI 时代，我真的非常感激。</p>

<p data-i18n="en">As you may have noticed, I came from an architecture school, where I spent years on design and aesthetic training. But more than that, the constant client-facing communication forced me to become someone who deeply values self-consistency. To convince a studio professor to accept a design idea, you have to walk in over-confident — even when, deep down, you're far from confident enough — and be ready to defend that idea with airtight, self-consistent logic. Getting to that level of confidence usually means going through a rough round of debating with myself first, then defending it all over again in studio crits with the professor. Most of the time, their pushback points to something real and worth iterating on, but sometimes I've gotta hold my ground. That process made me resilient — someone who can sit with a complicated project for a long time, keep iterating on an idea, and still stay self-aware throughout it. I think that's the real edge I picked up from architecture school.</p>
<p data-i18n="zh">你可能已经注意到，我本科是建筑学背景，曾花了好几年时间接受设计与美学训练。但更重要的是，那些持续不断的、面向"客户"（其实是评图老师）的沟通，逼着我成为一个非常看重逻辑自洽的人。想要说服评图老师接受一个设计想法，你必须表现得自信满满地走进教室——哪怕内心其实远没有那么有底气——并准备好用一套严密、自洽的逻辑去为这个想法辩护。要达到这种自信，通常意味着先要在自己脑子里经历一轮艰难的自我辩论，然后才能在评图现场再为它辩护一次。大多数时候，老师的质疑确实指出了值得迭代的真问题，但有时候我也得坚持自己的判断。这个过程把我磨炼得很有韧性——能够长时间陪伴一个复杂的项目，不断打磨一个想法，同时始终保持自我觉察。我认为这才是我从建筑学院真正带走的核心能力。</p>

<h2 data-i18n="en">The Unconventional Path</h2>
<h2 data-i18n="zh">一条非典型的路</h2>

<p data-i18n="en">Many people think it's pretty crazy to make such a big jump from architecture to data science — even my parents weren't on board at first. But I had real reasons for doing it, and looking back after two years of grinding through data science at Georgetown, I'm genuinely glad I did.</p>
<p data-i18n="zh">很多人觉得从建筑学跨到数据科学是个相当疯狂的决定——一开始连我父母都不太支持。但我做这个决定是有充分理由的，而在乔治城大学摸爬滚打了两年数据科学之后回头看，我真的很庆幸自己做了这个选择。</p>

<p data-i18n="en">The first reason comes from an observation I made during architecture school. Before starting any project, we're required to do a lot of upfront analysis — spatial analysis, activity analysis, even network analysis — meant to help us understand how a site is actually being used and how it could be improved. What I noticed, though, was that without more rigorous analytical tools, a lot of that work tended to stay at the level of compelling diagrams rather than concrete, measurable findings, and design decisions often came down to intuition and personal taste — which makes a lot of sense for a single building, where that kind of subjectivity is part of the craft. But I was surprised to see a similar pattern at the urban planning scale too, where decisions shaping entire communities still leaned heavily on precedent and the judgment of whoever was at the table. That's the gap I wanted to help close — by building up real analytical skills so that, eventually, I could bring more evidence into how these larger-scale design and planning decisions get made.</p>
<p data-i18n="zh">第一个原因来自我在建筑学院的一个观察。在开始任何项目之前，我们都要做大量的前期分析——场地分析、行为活动分析，甚至是路网分析——目的是理解一个场地实际是如何被使用的，以及它可以如何被改善。但我注意到，如果没有更严谨的分析工具，这些工作往往只能停留在"看起来很有说服力的图表"层面，而不是具体、可衡量的结论，最终的设计决策很大程度上还是依赖直觉和个人审美。对于单体建筑来说，这种主观性某种程度上本身就是这门手艺的一部分，是说得通的。但让我意外的是，在城市规划这个尺度上，同样的模式依然存在——那些影响整个社区的决策，很大程度上仍然依赖先例和在场者的个人判断。这正是我想去弥补的缺口：通过建立真正扎实的分析能力，让未来在做这些更大尺度的设计与规划决策时，能够引入更多基于证据的支持。</p>

<p data-i18n="en">The second reason comes from observing myself. Even though I've put a lot of effort into the design world, and I've developed a genuine hobby of constantly appreciating spaces and new ideas happening around the city, I realized over time that I lean more toward evidence-based thinking than toward ideas and emotions alone. I'm well aware that in a business setting, moving people with a grand vision and real emotion is genuinely important. But for me personally, architecture school gave me a lot more practice with ideas and emotional expression than with evidence-based, scientific rigor, and I wanted to round that out. So I went to Georgetown to pursue a Master's in data science.</p>
<p data-i18n="zh">第二个原因来自我对自己的观察。尽管我在设计这条路上投入了大量精力，也确实养成了一个爱好——持续关注城市里的空间和各种新想法——但随着时间推移，我意识到比起单纯依赖想法和情感表达，我其实更倾向于基于证据的思考方式。我也很清楚，在商业语境下，用宏大的愿景和真挚的情感去打动人心是非常重要的能力。但就我个人而言，建筑学院给我的更多是在想法和情感表达上的训练，而在基于证据的科学严谨性方面练习得相对较少，我想把这一块补齐。于是我来到乔治城大学攻读数据科学硕士。</p>

<p data-i18n="en">So at a glance, my path might look unconventional. But deep down, as I said, the logic behind this transition is fully self-consistent and justified to me — and I'm happy with where it's led.</p>
<p data-i18n="zh">所以乍一看，我的路径可能显得有点"非典型"。但正如前面所说，这次转变背后的逻辑对我来说是完全自洽、有理有据的——而且我也很满意它把我带到了今天这个地方。</p>

<h2 data-i18n="en">My Career Goals</h2>
<h2 data-i18n="zh">我的职业目标</h2>

<p data-i18n="en">In the near term, I'm looking for roles in data science, machine learning, or applied AI — places where I can keep sharpening the technical toolkit I built at Georgetown, from data pipelines to ML models to LLM-based systems.</p>
<p data-i18n="zh">短期来看，我希望从事数据科学、机器学习或应用 AI 相关的工作——在这些岗位上，我可以持续打磨在乔治城积累的技术能力，从数据管道、机器学习模型到基于大语言模型的系统。</p>

<p data-i18n="en">But the direction I'm most excited about long-term is bringing that toolkit back to the kinds of problems I cared about in architecture school — research, policy, or consulting work where evidence-based methods meet spatial, urban, or design-related questions. I don't see this as choosing data science over architecture; it's more like finally having the rigor to back up the intuition and design thinking I already had. Whether that's a research role studying how cities actually function, policy work that needs real data instead of guesswork, or consulting where technical analysis has to talk to design and planning stakeholders — that's where I think my background stops being two unrelated halves and becomes an actual advantage.</p>
<p data-i18n="zh">但从长期来看，我最期待的方向，是把这套技术能力带回我在建筑学院时就关心的那些问题——也就是那些将基于证据的方法与空间、城市或设计相关问题结合起来的研究、政策或咨询工作。我并不把这看作是"选择数据科学而放弃建筑学"，而更像是终于为我原本就有的直觉和设计思维找到了与之匹配的严谨方法论。无论是研究城市实际运作方式的科研岗位，需要真实数据而非主观猜测的政策工作，还是需要把技术分析与设计、规划相关方沟通的咨询工作——我相信在这些场景里，我的背景将不再是两个互不相关的部分，而会真正成为一种优势。</p>
